Afghan Cookies

Personal Connection

I was born in New Zealand, and when we lived there we would often make afghans cookies as a special treat. This recipe has been passed down in my family for generations, from my great grandmother. It came from a cookbook called: Edmonds Cookery Book, which is the second best selling book in New Zealand. Almost everyone who lived there had a copy of it at the time, and in my family it was passed down for generations until now we have this cookbook and the recipe that we make now is the same one that my great grandmother made many years ago.

History

No one really knows the history of afghan cookies but one of the theories that I've been seeing a lot is that they were baked for soldiers during the afghan war.

Instructions

In a medium sized bowl, cream the butter and sugar until light and fluffy.

Next add the flour and cocoa. Then stir into a creamed mixture.

Next mix the flour and cocoa.

After that add and carefully mix in the cornflakes.

Next you're going to grease an oven tray and add roughly tablespoon sized balls of the mixture onto the tray.

Then bake in the oven for 15 minutes at 180 degrees celsius.

In a separate bowl you’re going to add the chocolate, butter and cream and microwave it until melted.

After they have cooled, add the chocolate sauce on top of each cookie and add a walnut for decoration if you want.

Then the cookies are complete. I hope you enjoy!

Ingredients

Serving size:
  • Cookies:
  • 1 ½ cups butter, softened
  • ½ cup sugar
  • 1 ¼ cups standard plain flour
  • ¼ cup cocoa
  • 2 cups cornflakes
  • walnuts (optional)
  • Chocolate Icing:
  • 1 ½ cups chocolate
  • ¼ cup butter
  • ½ cup cream
